what_i_learned: >
  Today, I focused on environmental science and learned about PM25, which stands for particulate matter that is 2.5 micrometers or smaller in width. These tiny particles are a mix of solids and liquids floating in the air, and because they are so small, they can easily get deep into human lungs. I also learned that scientists measure PM2.5 concentration using the unit micrograms per cubic meter ($\mu g/m^3$).
  
  Additionally, I dug into atmospheric science and learned about the Planetary Boundary Layer, or PBL. The PBL is the very lowest part of the troposphere, which itself is the lowest layer of Earth's atmosphere. Since the PBL is closest to the ground, it is the exact area where human activity interacts with the weather and where most air pollution gets trapped.
