what_i_learned: |
  Today, I finalized our slide presentation and dedicated time to mapping out the physical connections between relative humidity (RH) and temperature. Understanding this relationship is critical for our model, as temperature dictates the water vapor capacity of the air. When temperature rises, the air's capacity to hold moisture increases, which causes the relative humidity to drop if the absolute moisture content remains constant. This inverse relationship heavily influences how aerosols expand or contract in the atmosphere.
  
  We went over how these thermodynamic changes directly impact Aerosol Optical Depth (AOD) retrievals and ground-level PM2.5 concentrations. Higher relative humidity can lead to hygroscopic growth, where soluble aerosol particles absorb moisture, swell in size, and alter the atmospheric light scattering measured by satellites. By clearly structuring these atmospheric interactions on our slides, we ensure our audience understands exactly why meteorological features are such high-importance predictors in our machine learning models.
