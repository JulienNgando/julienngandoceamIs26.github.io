what_i_learned: |
  Today, I wrapped up the final presentation slides for our research project, focusing heavily on how we contextualize our findings against existing literature. To achieve this, I created comparative visualizations that map out how our deep learning models performed when predicting $PM_{2.5}$ concentrations relative to benchmarks established in previous studies. Synthesizing these metrics side-by-side allowed us to highlight where our architectural adjustments and temporal feature engineering either outperformed or aligned with published research, giving our results a solid foundation within the broader atmospheric modeling community.

  Beyond just showing raw performance differences, I tailored the visual layout to clearly break down the key parameters driving those variations, such as site locations, time intervals, and feature sets. Illustrating these cross-study comparisons made it much easier to tell a coherent story about our work: while our $R^2$ scores and error metrics demonstrate competitive baseline accuracy, the visual comparison clearly illustrates how temporal granularity and input features alter model behavior across different environmental datasets. With these final comparative slides in place, the slide deck feels complete, polished, and ready for our upcoming presentation.
blockers: |
  Aligning the scale and performance metrics from outside literature with our own internal benchmark results was surprisingly tricky. Many papers reported error metrics using different units, variations of RMSE/MAE, or evaluated across non-equivalent prediction horizons. Spending time re-scaling those baseline figures so they could be directly and fairly plotted alongside our 30-minute and 60-minute $PM_{2.5}$ model outputs took up a huge chunk of the day.
reflection: |
  Finishing the final slides is an awesome milestone—it forces you to step back from the raw code and appreciate the full scope of what was built over the past few weeks. Placing our deep learning results directly alongside established literature gave me a much stronger appreciation for how our work fits into the larger scientific picture. It feels great to see all the data processing, model debugging, and paper drafting come together into a compelling narrative, and I feel confident heading into the final presentation phase!
