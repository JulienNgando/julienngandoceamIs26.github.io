what_i_learned: >
  Today I focused on enhancing my machine learning models through Feature Engineering and Feature Selection. Feature Engineering involves creating new predictive variables to give the model more context, while Feature Selection narrows down the dataset to the most impactful features that correlate strongly with the target variable. To expand my dataset, I utilized time-series techniques including lagging and rolling windows. Lagging allowed me to capture historical context by shifting data points from previous time steps, while rolling window features captured dynamic trends by calculating statistical metrics, such as the moving average and standard deviation, over set intervals.

  After augmenting the dataset, I reran my models to evaluate the impact of these new variables on overall performance. Through this process, I discovered a definitive "sweet spot" when training the models on the top 40 selected features. To identify these high-priority inputs, I utilized Mutual Information scoring, which measures the statistical dependence between each feature and the target variable to determine how much information a specific feature shares with the outcome.
