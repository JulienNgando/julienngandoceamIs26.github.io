what_i_learned: |
  Attending the AWS Summit today was an incredible experience that offered a massive window into how enterprise tech operates at scale. A major highlight was spending time on the expo floor connecting directly with industry representatives. I had a fantastic conversation with the team at IBM regarding their latest hybrid cloud architectures and how they deploy enterprise-level AI models. Right after, I stopped by the Duracell booth, which was surprisingly fascinating—we talked about the intersection of IoT infrastructure, edge computing, and industrial-grade power solutions required to keep remote sensors running seamlessly in the field. 

  Beyond networking, the sessions themselves were highly impactful. I attended a deep-dive talk focused on the critical relationship between Cybersecurity and AI. The speakers broke down how modern engineering teams are utilizing machine learning tools to predict and intercept vulnerabilities in real-time, while simultaneously addressing the unique security challenges that come with deploying LLMs and large data pipelines. It definitely shifted how I think about safeguarding data workflows from the ground up.
blockers: |
  On the technical side, I hit a bit of a bottleneck trying to sync my local data processing pipeline with our cloud storage buckets. I'm running into some frustrating IAM (Identity and Access Management) permission errors and configuration mismatches with the AWS CLI. It is slowing down my ability to run large-scale testing automation, so sorting out these credential pathways is my top priority for tomorrow morning.
reflection: |
  Stepping out of the day-to-day coding bubble to attend the summit was exactly what I needed. It’s easy to get hyper-focused on solving immediate, isolated engineering bugs, but seeing how massive companies orchestrate their cloud infrastructure forces you to look at the bigger picture. Moving forward, I want to be much more intentional about designing scalable code rather than just making things work in a local environment.
