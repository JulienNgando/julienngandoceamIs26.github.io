what_i_learned: |
  Building on last week's theoretical research, today I transitioned into the implementation phase by building and training Recurrent Neural Network (RNN), Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM), and Gated Recurrent Unit (GRU) models. The primary goal was to take sequential data and evaluate how effectively these deep learning architectures capture temporal trends compared to standard baseline models. Managing the sequence length and properly reshaping the input tensors into the required three-dimensional format (samples, time steps, features) was critical to getting the network layers to communicate smoothly.

  To quantify and compare model performance, I tracked Mean Absolute Error (MAE) and the Coefficient of Determination ($R^2$ score) across all architectures. Tracking MAE provided a direct, interpretable measure of the average error magnitude in the predictions, while the $R^2$ score allowed me to evaluate how much of the underlying variance the deep learning models were actually explaining. Comparing these metrics side-by-side highlighted the clear advantages of the gated mechanisms in LSTMs and GRUs, as they consistently achieved tighter error bounds and higher predictive accuracy over the standard RNN.
blockers: |
  The main hurdle today was debugging the tensor shapes within the data pipeline to ensure they matched the strict input requirements of the recurrent layers. I ran into repeated dimensions mismatch errors during the batching process when trying to feed multiple parallel meteorological and satellite features into the LSTM units. It took a systematic review of the array reshaping functions and sequence padding to get the data loader to execute without crashing.
reflection: |
  Moving from theoretical equations to functional code always exposes minor implementation nuances that reading alone can't teach. Seeing the actual impact of LSTMs and GRUs over standard RNNs in real-time performance metrics underscores why architectural design matters so much for sequential datasets. Moving forward, I want to focus on hyperparameter tuning—specifically adjusting the lookback windows and dropout rates—to optimize these scores even further.
