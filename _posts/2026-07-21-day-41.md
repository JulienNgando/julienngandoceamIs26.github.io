what_i_learned: |
  Today was all about putting the final polish on our project presentation, specifically focusing on how we present the ultimate face-off between our traditional Machine Learning models (like XGBoost, LightGBM, and CatBoost) and our Deep Learning architectures (LSTM, Bi-GRU, RNN). To make these comparisons intuitive for the audience, I crafted clear comparative plots that map out key performance metrics like $R^2$, RMSE, and MAE across different time intervals and monitoring sites. Visualizing the data side-by-side really highlighted where the tree-based ensemble methods held their ground versus where the sequential deep learning models pulled ahead in catching temporal patterns.

  Beyond just generating the graphs, I spent time designing the layout of these final slides to tell a clear, compelling story. I structured the visualizations to emphasize how features like lagged inputs and temporal granularity influenced both model families differently. Distilling weeks of complex modeling experiments and hyperparameter tuning down to clean, high-impact visuals ensures that when we present our findings, the research group can immediately digest the core takeaways without getting lost in the raw numbers.
blockers: |
  Selecting the right visual scale to fairly represent both model families on a single slide without cluttering the screen proved surprisingly tricky. Fine-tuning Matplotlib and Seaborn plot parameters—like balancing bar widths, adjusting font sizes for legibility, and aligning dual-axis charts—took multiple revisions to get everything looking clean and presentation-ready.
reflection: |
  Translating high-dimensional modeling results into clean, accessible visuals is almost an art form in itself. It is one thing to know your models inside and out from the code side, but presenting those insights clearly to an audience requires a completely different mindset. Reaching this milestone feels like a major accomplishment, and seeing all our hard work come together in a polished final deck makes all the debugging and fine-tuning over the past few weeks totally worth it.
