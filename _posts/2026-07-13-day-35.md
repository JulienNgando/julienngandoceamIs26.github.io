what_i_learned: |
  Today was all about wrapping up our final group demo video, taking it from rough screen recordings to a polished, final edit. In the presentation, we walked through the core of our research, specifically focusing on PM2.5—particulate matter less than 2.5 micrometers in diameter—and its significant environmental and cardiovascular health impacts. We structured the narrative to explain how these microscopic particles bypass our natural respiratory defenses to enter the bloodstream, driving home why building highly accurate machine learning models to predict these air quality spikes is so critical.

  In addition to defining the physical science, we showcased our team's technical workflow, demonstrating how our various models—including the corrected RNN—perform when forecasting these dangerous shifts. Editing the video required balancing high-level atmospheric science explanations with the code-heavy details of our machine learning pipeline. It was highly satisfying to sync up our voiceovers with clean, dynamic visual demonstrations of our $R^2$ performance graphs and watch the entire project's story come together.
blockers: |
  My biggest hurdle today was simply maintaining focus while sitting through hours of rendering and tedious audio-syncing. Sitting at a desk matching audio tracks to fast-moving screen recordings is a slow, repetitive process, and it was tough to keep my concentration sharp after the fifteenth playback.
reflection: |
  Putting this video together really made me appreciate how important science communication is. We can build the most elegant, leak-free predictive models in the world, but their real value lies in how effectively we can explain their significance to others. Condensing weeks of data engineering, index debugging, and model training into a concise, engaging visual story was a challenging but incredibly rewarding way to synthesize everything we have accomplished.
