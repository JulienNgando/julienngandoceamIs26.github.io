what_i_learned: |
  Today marked a massive milestone as I officially finished writing my assigned sections for the final draft of our research paper. I focused on documenting our methodology, detailing our model architecture comparisons, and cleanly laying out our preprocessing steps—including the sliding window configurations and the feature engineering logic we worked so hard to correct last week. Having all of that complex implementation detail down on paper feels incredibly rewarding, and it is satisfying to see the narrative of our research finally come together in a cohesive, academic format.

  Alongside drafting the manuscript, I spent time polishing our visual assets to ensure they meet publication standards. Specifically, I recreated and refactored the plotting scripts for our Mean Absolute Error (MAE) comparison graphs. The new visualizations are much cleaner, with improved label scaling, consistent color schemes matching our paper's theme, and clear error bars that accurately represent the predictive performance of our FNN, CNN, and RNN models. Having high-quality figures really elevates the draft and makes our performance differences immediately interpretable.
blockers: |
  Ensuring the formatting and styling of the recreated MAE graphs perfectly matched the LaTeX document layout was surprisingly tedious. I kept running into minor scaling issues where the axis labels would get cut off in the exported PDF figures, which required me to manually fine-tune the matplotlib bounding box parameters and figure dimensions.
reflection: |
  Transitioning from active coding and debugging to formal academic writing forces you to look at your own work from a completely different perspective. Explaining complex data engineering decisions and model trade-offs clearly on paper really solidifies your own understanding of the work. We still have some collective editing and fine-tuning ahead as a team, but crossing the finish line on this draft makes the end of the research cycle feel incredibly real.
