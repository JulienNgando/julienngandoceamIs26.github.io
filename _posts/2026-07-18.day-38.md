what_i_learned: |
  Today, I focused on drafting the Results and Discussion section of our research paper, specifically analyzing the performance metrics compiled in Table IV. I structured the first part of this section to compare how our different deep learning architectures—including LSTM and Bi-GRU models—behaved when predicting $PM_{2.5}$ concentrations at the Howard and Beltsville monitoring sites. By pointing out that the $R^2$ scores clustered tightly between 0.528 and 0.544 for Howard and 0.597 to 0.606 for Beltsville at the 60-minute mark, I was able to demonstrate to our research group that architectural complexity plays a secondary role when the models are restricted to historical, autoregressive input features.

  In the second paragraph of this draft, I framed the comparative advantages of the recurrent models while introducing the impact of temporal resolution. I highlighted that while advanced sequential models like LSTM and Bi-GRU consistently edged out the simpler baselines with slightly higher $R^2$ values and lower error margins, these variations were ultimately marginal. Instead, I directed the reader's attention to the transition from 60-minute to 30-minute intervals as the primary catalyst for performance shifts. Writing this segment allowed us to clearly communicate that temporal granularity and data structure, rather than just raw model selection, are the most critical factors in optimizing these atmospheric predictive pipelines.
blockers: |

Spending hours debugging compiler errors and adjusting column widths in the code definitely slowed down my actual writing momentum today.
reflection: |
  Synthesizing raw simulation metrics into an academic narrative is always a great exercise in critical thinking. It forces you to look past the raw numbers and ask *why* the models are behaving this way under the hood—like realizing that a model's temporal context is far more influential than whether you use a GRU versus a standard LSTM. Translating these technical observations into precise, scientific prose is definitely challenging, but it makes the final research paper feel incredibly solid.
